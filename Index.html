Before you begin

What this is — and what it is not

The PRF-22 is a structured reflection prompt set, not a validated psychometric scale. It gives you a provisional map of how you tend to perceive moral situations — what you notice first, what registers as urgent, and where your perception may have characteristic blind spots. It is a beginning, not a verdict.

Two parts. Six scenario questions that reveal your moral foundations weighting. Six assembly history questions about the experiences and relationships that shaped your moral perception. The more honest and specific your answers, the more the profile will feel like genuine recognition rather than a generic description.
What you receive. Your Philosophical Profile — a 600–800 word portrait of your moral architecture generated from your answers. A send button that carries your profile directly into the Navigator with a single code. And two downloadable JSON files for use with different reflection platforms.
Which path is yours? Track 1 (with AI): Complete both parts, generate your Philosophical Profile, and carry it into the Navigator — your Twin will begin knowing who you are. Track 2 (no AI): Complete both parts, download your answers using the button at the bottom of Part 2, and write a 300-word personal reflection essay for Canvas. Both tracks are equally valid. Your instructor will tell you which is required for your section.

Privacy & Data Disclosure

What this tool is

The PRF-22 Gateway is an educational reflection tool for the Ethics Beyond Boundaries course at Saint Petersburg College. It is not a mental health service, psychological assessment, clinical instrument, or substitute for counseling. The PRF-22 is a structured reflection prompt set, not a validated psychometric scale. Results are provisional and are intended to open inquiry, not to label or define you.

What stays in your browser

Your scenario answers and essay responses are stored only in your browser’s memory while you use the app. Nothing is sent to any server before you click Generate Profile. If you close the tab before generating, your answers are not saved anywhere.

What is transmitted when you generate your profile

When you click Generate Profile, your six scenario answers and six essay responses travel via an encrypted HTTPS/TLS connection to a course proxy server on Render.com, operated for this course. The proxy forwards your request to Anthropic’s Claude API, which generates your profile text and returns it to your browser.

No copy of your essay responses or generated profile is retained on the proxy server or by Anthropic. This is a one-time transmission at the moment of generation.

What is transmitted when you send to The Navigator

When you click “Begin with The Navigator,” your PRF-22 profile data is sent via HTTPS to the course relay endpoint on Render.com. It is held in server memory only — never written to disk — for up to 10 minutes, then deleted whether or not it is received. The moment The Navigator retrieves it with your code, it is deleted immediately from server memory. The code can only be used once.

AI training — what Anthropic does and does not do

Anthropic does not use API data to train its models. Your essay responses and generated profile are not used for model training, improvement, or retention by Anthropic. See anthropic.com/privacy.

Your instructor and Saint Petersburg College

Your instructor cannot see your PRF-22 answers, essay responses, or generated profile through this app. Nothing is reported to SPC, Canvas, or any institutional system. If you share your profile as part of a course activity, that sharing is your choice and is initiated by you.

What to write — and what not to write

Write only what you are comfortable transmitting to a third-party AI service. You do not need to include identifying information. The more specific your answers, the more precise your profile — but you remain in control of what you share.

If you are in crisis

If you are experiencing thoughts of harming yourself or others, please call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, 24/7 in the US) or contact a mental health professional immediately. This app cannot provide crisis support.

Voluntary participation

Completing the PRF-22 is a course activity, but what you write is your own. You are not required to share your profile or JSON files unless your instructor explicitly asks and you choose to participate.
